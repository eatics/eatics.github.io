IDENTIFYING THE PROBLEM

Brainstorming

First of all, the entire team has gathered for a brainstorming session where each team member exposed a problem that they are facing on a daily basis. We decided that one of the most important aspects of our lives is represented by our eating habits, the way we manage, prepare and eat our food.

Main issues

  • People have less and less time dedicated to cooking
  • People keep buying products and forget about other ingredients that are already in the fridge
  • People tend to throw away a lot of food because they don’t manage the things they hold in the fridge properly
  • People who are not experienced with cooking (us included) don’t bother too much with preparing a healthy meal and instead, they order the food they want to eat

All these problems lead to an unhealthy lifestyle, which can harm us all in the long run To strengthen our observations, each team member discussed with their family and close friends regarding their eating habits and the quantity of the food and ingredients they wasted in the past couple of months. Their insights matched the issues we previously identified and made us go deeper into those problems and into finding a solution for them.

INITIAL SOLUTION

We thought that a web application capable of offering you recipes based on the products you already have in the fridge, taking into account the expire dates of each product in order to reduce food waste would be a great start in solving the main problems that we had found before.

Also, we came up with some interesting possible features of our application, which would transform our solution into an all-in-one smart kitchen assistant, capable of guiding us through the whole process, from the moment we decide what we need to buy to the moment when we cook our dishes in an efficient and easy way.

PROCESS DESCRIPTION IN DETAIL

Customer Segments

After the initial discussion in which we identified the main problems, we started from the premise that anyone who want to eat healthy and prepare their meals by themselves without wasting the food they already have could be a good candidate for using our application.


How we reached them

  • Face-to-face interviews with family members
  • Online interviews with close friends
  • Online form in Romanian (Google Form), shared on social media (Facebook and WhatsApp)



How we collected information

  • Automatically via Google Forms
  • Anonymous answers
  • From spreadsheets
  • Generated relevant charts



Results

Almost 85% of the participants said that they prefer home-cooked meals.


Most participants consider that they are at a medium to low level in terms of cooking.


We observe that only 15% cook daily while almost a third of the participants cook 2-3 times a week. About a quarter cook once a week and another quarter cook just once a month. A few procent don't cook at all.


Most of the participants prepare eazy or intermediate dishes and only ~11% try to cook elaborate food. A few procent don't cook at all (~6%).


We can see that more than half of the people we questioned are not sure about what they want to cook on a daily basis.


We observe that two thirds of the participants know exactly what to cook before they go shopping and approximately one third decide what to cook with the ingredients they bought.


More than 75% of the people that participated in this form are struggling in finding a recipe they want to cook, as it takes them more than five minutes to make a decision.


Most of the people like to cook traditional dishes but also try new ones.


Almost 35% of the participants never discuss new recipes with their friends or family and, on average, most rarely discuss.


Almost half of the participants throw away rotten food weekly, 20% throw away 2-3 times a week or more often while a quarter throw away food monthly.


It is observable that people usually know what ingredients they have. Some of them less often than the others.


Primary devices of participants are the laptop and the smartphone. Only a few of them have tablet or smart fridge.


We covered all age ranges, but the vast majority of the people that answered were between 18 and 45.
REFINING THE SOLUTION

After a series of general questions to see what is the main customer segment that we tend to reach into, a series of conclusions have been taken. Firstly, over 85% of the participants prefer home-cooked meals. This shows that most of them are opened to a new recipe that our app would propose to them. We also noticed that the majority of people often choose eazy and intermediate dishes, in accordance with their cooking skill (mostly amateurs or people who cook for themselves or for friends and family). So, we will focus on introducing a majority of accessible dishes in our app, but also some challenging ones for those who want to improve also

As a generality that is encouraging for our idea, we saw that people are not that afraid to cook as we though, especially that the age interval with the most participants was 18 to 25 (especially students who start their new alone life, far from their parents and realize the importance of being able to avoid famine). Half of them don’t know what to cook on a daily basis, and one of our main features is meant to resolve this issue. The users will discover a new recipe any time he wants to.

Although two thirds of them have an idea of what they want to cook before they go shopping, more than 75% of them are struggling to think on an average of more than 5 minutes to decide what they will cook with the remaining ingredients. This is also covered by our strategy of reducing the food waste and tracking the food that you still have and try to recommend a recipe that uses only those exact ingredients or with as few as possible in addition (some common ones like water or salt or flour).

One of the main aspects is that people in our segment seems to be that even if they like to cook traditional dishes they are also inclined to new discoveries, but it is shown that those come rarely from discussions with family and often from searches on the internet or video tutorials on cooking blogs or sites or channels.

From the point of view of throwing the expired ingredients, people don’t have an automatic way to track the expiration dates of products and even they usually know what they have in the fridge, they could be surprised with some expired products. We want to implement a feature that notifies you when some ingredients are going to expire and in which easy recipes you could include it immediately.

The most common devices for our participants are pc, laptops and smartphones, so we will try to make a cross-platform app that is accessible from all of these devices so you won’t be restricted to only one.

We gained additional motivation for moving forward with some of the features we thought in the beginning but we also received some new ideas from our interviewees. Some of the most relevant for us that could be even included in our product are:

  • a smart assistant that will guide the user through the main steps of cooking a recipe
  • display of a list of all macro-nutrients of the plate cooked
  • a personalized subset of recipes that are suggested for you to try, like the ones you already cooked and a rating system of them, and the list of what you need to buy on your first visit at the supermarket
  • link-up with all delivery apps when you want to get a recipe faster when you have an empty fridge and you can’t go to market
CUSTOMER VALIDATION

The previous questionnaire showed us that our solution is a viable option to the problems people are facing regarding their eating and cooking habits.

They also recommended us some of the features that we had already thought of implementing them within our application, confirming us once more that eatics could be a great solution for them.

Furthermore, we managed to identify the proper age range and habits of our potential customers and then we sent them another questionnaire that would validate our solution for our users.

Solution validation

As in the results of the other survey, most of the people have a laptop and a smartphone. Some of them use tablets and only a few percent have smart fridge.
Most participants used more than 5 applications in the last month.
Almost 40% of participants use apps for food and drink
Most people tend to use applications on multiple platforms several times a month.
Almost 70% of the participants have between 1 and 3 devices connected to their phone.
Most participants (almost 60%) don’t use the voice assistant on smart devices.
Most of the participants throw food at least once a week.
We observe that almost 25% of participants would not pay for a smart kitchen assistant, but almost 30% would pay up to 10 RON, and another 38% would pay even up to 50 RON
We can observe a significant decrease in the age of the participants.

Some of the questions from the customer validation form had short answer text, and we noticed the following:

  • Most participants do not use any tools to manage their products. They try to keep in mind or simply look them into the fridge.
  • When people doesn’t know what to cook, they often order from restaurants or search for a new idea on the internet.
  • Some participants are satisfied with their way of organizing the products, but most would like to cook more and throw less food.
  • Some of participants came with various suggestions and observations including:
    • a keep-tracker for products like sugar and flour (products that are not kept in the fridge)
    • an extension of application that can retain the number of calories for a healthy lifestyle

Conclusion:

After the interpretation of the collected data, the conclusion is that the proposed solution is reliable and has its potential users in the age range 18-40. The main features such as the recipe suggestion and food tracking have been validated by the plots above. The connection with a smart fridge is not a valid option because most of the people don't have one. The solution’s features can be sold as a subscription, subscribers receiving some additional features then free users.
